從「成長」邁向「品質」
此文件正式確立 CVE 計畫「成長時代」與即將到來「品質時代」的分野。根據 CISA 說法，CVE 成長時代「成功招募超過 460 個 CVE 編號授權機構組成的全球網絡，使網路安全社群在識別、定義和建檔數十萬個資安漏洞的能力達到指數級成長」。

然而，該計畫現在必須演進以「滿足全球網路安全社群需求」。因此，CVE 計畫需轉向新發展重點，特別是提升信任度、加快回應速度並強化資安漏洞資料品質。

這種轉變並非新概念。去年 9 月，時任 CISA 資安漏洞應變與協調品牌主管 Lindsey Cerkovnik 曾表示：「過去八到十年間，CVE 計畫處於成長時代，主要致力於增加 CNA 數量和資安漏洞揭露數量。現在我們正進入品質時代，專注於提升資料品質，改善整個生態系統。」

CISA 執行副主任 Christopher Butera 在今年 8 月美國黑帽資安大會演講時也呼應這個觀點，強調資安漏洞揭露過程需要更多自動化機制：「我們必須在整個生態系統建置自動化功能，加速漏洞修補流程。現在我們正從成長時代邁向品質時代。」[^3]

## 希望-實際

### 第一面向 Market & product design fixes

1. **Secure‑by‑design & defaults**

   * Ship software with secure defaults, automatic safe updates, and minimal attack surface.
2. **Bug bounties & vulnerability markets**

   * Legitimize vulnerability discovery via coordinated disclosure and fair bug‑bounty payments (reduces incentive to sell to black market).
3. **Data minimization & encryption**

   * Store less data; use strong encryption at rest and in transit; design systems where stolen data is less useful (tokenization, vaults).
4. **Standardized security signals**

   * Machine‑readable security attestations (like an SBOM or security score) to let customers compare products on security.

### C. Practical ideas to reduce the imbalance (prioritized, action‑oriented)

### Short/mid-term technical & operational steps (companies & product teams)

1. **Reduce blast radius**

   * Use least privilege, microsegmentation, zero‑trust network principles.

3. **Rapid patching & vulnerability management**

   * Adopt risk‑based patching (critical exposures first), continuous vulnerability scanning.
   * Use feature flags / phased rollouts to reduce update risk.

4. **Supply chain security**

   * Enforce SBOMs (software bill of materials), vet vendors, require minimum security baselines in contracts.

5. **Credential hygiene**

   * Enforce MFA, remove permanent credentials, rotate keys, and limit privileged accounts.

6. **Threat intelligence sharing**

   * Join ISAC/ISAO or industry groups to share indicators and TTPs; automate IOC ingestion.

### E. Broader strategic shifts that would change the economics

* **Increase attacker cost / risk**: stronger international law enforcement, sanctions on infrastructure providers, and tighter crypto traceability.
* **Reduce attacker revenue**: restrict resale markets (seize marketplaces; force exchanges to KYC), make stolen data less useful (tokenization), and improve detection to reduce theft windows.
* **Shift incentives**: hold manufacturers and vendors more accountable for insecure products so costs of insecurity are internalized.

Separate Permission from Information
One common pitfall in most security systems is to confuse the granting of permission
(authentication and authorization to do something, such as make a purchase) with the
possession of information that uniquely identifies a client. Names and birth dates are personal
information that identify a person and can be used for identity theft. Credit card numbers have
a similar value to criminals, even though they provide little information beyond some credit

Institute an Incentive/Reward Structure
Today, compliance is a big driver in the adoption of security technologies. Several other authors
discuss it in this book. However, compliance is centered on penalties: if you are not compliant,
there will be a price to pay. There is very little incentive structure to reward good behavior.

Establish a Social Metric and Reputation System for Data Responsibility
Just as “greenness” measures a company’s commitment to the environment, we need an
analogous metric that measures the company’s maturity in its data-handling operations. And
just as greenness can help a company achieve social goodwill, a good data security reputation
should result in customer royalty and heightened trust from business partners. Perhaps such
a metric and reputation framework would make firms more inclined to internalize some of the
external cost, if it will help them garner a more favorable reputation.
